Strategy Planning for the Fall 2024 Election

The "Effort Remaining" Metric

I'm trying to develop an "Effort Remaining" concept to measure our progress in a ward, city, or county.

I begin with a concept of a "win number" defined as 53% of a weighted average of 3x the total votes in the 2012, 2016, and 2020 Presidential elections, and the 2028 and 2022 Governor's elections.

That win number will be harder to attain in some places and easier in others, so we need a concempt of a "lift". The "lift" is 1 / the probability of a Democrat being elected in that ward, averaged over the elections of 2012, 2014, 2016, 2018, and 2020. "Lift" should always be equal to or greater than 1.0. It acts as a coefficient when, applied to the "win number", creates a measure of the effort necessary (as a count of voter commitments) to achieve the win number. The "effort remaining" is that measure minus Biden's vote count in 2020.
